To all SoC Freshmen,

Welcome to the School of Computing! You’ve already started the first of what’s going to be an immense four years in university. The ride is going to be arduous, caffeinated, adrenaline-filled, and, we hope, fun.

At NUS Hackers, we’re looking to inject the adrenaline and fun of computer science through hacker culture. The aim is to empower people to solve problems using tech like the hassle in planning your module timetable, create their own projects (e.g. NUSMods in 2013) , and contribute to popular open-source projects, (e.g. NUSMods today).

At the same time, computing can be daunting for freshmen. We’ve found that freshmen could use guidance about the long-term: what they’re looking to achieve in SoC, and how; and advice about the short-term: choosing which modules suit them, deciding which field to specialize, crafting a study plan, navigating internships, and more.

As such, NUS Hackers is launching Project Mentor, a semester-long mentorship program, to help computing freshmen gain exposure to hacker culture, and transition to SoC life in their first semester.

As part of our pilot, we’re looking to match around 35 mentees with 35 mentors. In particular, we will pair two mentors to two mentees to allow for richer discussion and to make it less daunting to ask for advice. The mentors are seniors or alumni from SoC who are passionate about hacking, and about helping freshmen do well at SoC.

You can sign up for the program here: https://nus.campuslabs.com/engage/submitter/form/start/499264. Sign ups will be open between this Friday 10:00AM, 27th August 2021, to next Friday 10:00AM, 3rd September 2021. If your application is successful, you will be contacted by your mentor within a week, after we have finalized the groupings.

Do note the following:

This program is only open to freshmen enrolling at the School of Computing.

Mentees will be selected through random balloting from the sign-ups. There is no first-come-first-serve priority.

The programme will start in the second week or third week of September and end in December.

Further questions may be directed to coreteam@nushackers.org.
